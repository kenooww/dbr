01 — The cove

A resort built around one tide chart

Amihan Cove sits on a quiet stretch of coastline where the trade winds roll in cool and steady. There's no entertainment schedule here — just a reef at low tide, a hammock at high tide, and a kitchen that cooks whatever the boats brought in that morning.

Thirty-two rooms, four room types, one shared shoreline. That's the whole plan.

03 — On the grounds

Everything the cove keeps stocked

Reef snorkeling

Gear at the boathouse, reef fifty meters from shore, guided trips at dawn.

Bicycles & kayaks

Free rentals for guests — the village market is a fifteen-minute ride down the coast road.

Cove-side grill

Open-air dinner service facing the sunset; the day's catch decides the menu.

Tide-pool spa

Treatments timed to low tide, in an open pavilion built over the rock pools.

Sailing & boat trips

Half-day trips to the outer islands aboard the resort's two outrigger boats.

Stargazing deck

No light pollution past the tree line — telescopes come out most clear nights.
